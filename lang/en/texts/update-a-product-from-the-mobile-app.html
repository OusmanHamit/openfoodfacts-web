Take missing photos

You need the Android Open Food Facts app to update a product that way. You can get it there.

1. Check for any photo or data mismatch

Scan any product using its barcode. Check if the front photo seems to match the current packaging. You can slide up to compare all the values and photos.

Open the product to send new photos

Take a new front photo

Open Food Facts will propose to crop the photo. Prefer a wide crop to a narrow crop as this can be adjusted later by you or another contributor. We don’t have to lose any valuable product information.

Take a new ingredients photo

Hint: If you are in a rush, you can take a wide photo of all the ingredients in all languages and it will be possible to crop more precisely later in all languages, even if you have thrown away the packaging.

Take a new nutrition photo

You can now repeat the process with the nutrition table. Usually nutrition tables are multilingual. So you can limit yourself to the main language of the product. If the nutrition table is in the form of a separate text, you can of course make a different photo crop for each language.

Well done !

5. Updating outdated nutrition values and ingredient lists.

Now we need to make sure that the values for ingredients and nutrition are consistent with the new photos we took.

Opening the edit mode

Updating ingredients by performing a new text extraction

Alternatively, you can use the 2 circular cursors that appeared to select all the ingredients, but by experience, that is more complex to do.

Checking the ingredients carefully before validating them

Then check carefully but each ingredient matches what's on the packaging or on the photo. Again you can tap the photo to zoom it.

  • Take extra care about the little stars that may indicate specific things about the ingredients. Ensure that the OCR hasn't converted it to an apostrophe or something else.
  • Ensure that commas have been properly transcribed.
  • Ensure that there is no missing ingredient or typo.

Be careful: at this point, the ingredients haven't been saved !

Saving immediately your work, or moving on to editing nutrition

To save them you have two options:

  • The top arrow makes sense if you want to just edit the ingredients.
  • Going to the nutrition tab makes sense if you need to adjust values there as well.
